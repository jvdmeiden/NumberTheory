Greatest Common Divisors

Related to divisibility are 'Greatest Common Divisors'. The greatest common divisor of two (or more) numbers is defined as: the largest number that divides those numbers. The Greatest Common Divisor of 10 and 15 - notation GCD(10,15)- for instance is 5.
The following graph shows if the Greatest Common Divisor of an integer pair is larger than one (dark squares). Through this graph shimmer numerous patterns:



  • the diagonal stands out as a solid line, rather obvious number X and X itself have GCD X
  • primes stand out as vertical - and horizontal - relatively white lines
  • Y=2*X stands out as a straight line
  • lots and lots of other patterns appear!
